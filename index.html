Questions about research papers

Instructions (click to toggle visibility)

We are trying to improve NLP paper reading experience for readers of all backgrounds. Towards this goal, we would like to know what kinds of questions a reader might have about a paper after they have read its title and abstract, and we seek your help in obtaining these questions. Thank you for helping us! Below you will see titles and abstracts of papers recently posted in CS.CL section of ArXiV. We ask you to read each title and abtract completely, and type in questions you think would be answered by the paper. In addition, we also ask you to select options from three drop-down menus below to help us understand your background, and how much you know about the paper. Please keep the following in mind:

  • Please write specific questions. Most questions that you expect to be answered with a "yes" or "no", or with a phrase or a sentence (or two) from the paper are good questions. Click on "Example questions" below to look at some examples.
  • Click "Add Question" after you are done typing a question about the paper. You can "Edit" or "Delete" previously added questions any time.
  • You have 10 paper abstracts to choose from, and you can skip papers if you cannot think of questions for some of them.
  • Please leave any feeedback you have for us in the box at the bottom of this page.

Title: Learning to Reason With Adaptive Computation

Abstract: Multi-hop inference is necessary for machine learning systems to successfully solve tasks such as Recognising Textual Entailment and Machine Reading. In this work, we demonstrate the effectiveness of adaptive computation for learning the number of inference steps required for examples of different complexity and that learning the correct number of inference steps is difficult. We introduce the first model involving Adaptive Computation Time which provides a small performance benefit on top of a similar model without an adaptive component as well as enabling considerable insight into the reasoning process of the model.

Here are some questions one might ask about the paper based on this abstract:

  1. What is their baseline model?
  2. Do they use reinforcement learning?
  3. What textual entailment datasets do they evaluate ACT on?
  4. How do they define the notion of correct number of inference steps?
  5. Do they evaluate only on English datasets? #BenderRule
